Population Trends Between Countries and Continents.

Why?

We want to understand past trends between population density and urban population and utilize them to help predict future social and economic issues. We also want to understand linkage of inter-continental issues — natural, economical, social — with population density, fertility rate and median age, and predict future irregularities.

How?

We used interactive visualizations and regression models to evaluate population trends and their relationship with fertility, median age, population density, etc. We also predicted future trends in these factors (population, density, fertility, etc.)

Read Our Story

Data Analysis

We utilized several datasets from Kaggle and analyzed the data using Python and Pandas. After that, we created visuals using Plotly Express to emphasize important relationships. View these visuals on our Visuals Page. Listed below are the datasets we analyzed.

  • World Continents
    • Fertility Rates, Median Age, Density, Net Migrants, Urban Population, and Year
  • 25 Most Populated Countries
    • China, India, Germany, Nigeria, Mexico, USA, Japan, Iran, etc.
    • Fertility Rate, Median Age, Urban Population, Net Migrants, and Year
  • Women in Labor Force
    • Data from 1990s to present
    • Shows the percentage of women who are in labor force

Machine Learning and Beyond

We created several accurate Machine Learning Models, such as SVR, Lasso Regression, Polynomial Regression, and Gaussian Regression, from SciKit Learn. These display excellent metrics and the ability to predict future population trends. Learn more on our Machine Learning Page.
